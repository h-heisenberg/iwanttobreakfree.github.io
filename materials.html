3D Printing Materials Guide

Choose the right material for your project. We offer a wide range of FDM filaments and resin materials with different properties to suit various applications and requirements.

FDM Filament Materials

Commonly available thermoplastics for Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) printing

PLA (Polylactic Acid)

The most popular and beginner-friendly 3D printing material. Made from renewable resources like corn starch, PLA is biodegradable and easy to print with minimal warping.

Print Temp
190-220°C
Bed Temp
40-60°C
Operating Temp
-40°C to 60°C
Best For:
  • Prototypes and concept models
  • Educational projects
  • Decorative items and displays
  • Beginner 3D printing

ABS (Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene)

Strong, durable, and heat-resistant plastic commonly used in injection molding. ABS parts can be post-processed by sanding, painting, or gluing.

Print Temp
220-250°C
Bed Temp
80-110°C
Operating Temp
-40°C to 80°C
Best For:
  • Functional parts and tools
  • Automotive components
  • Electronic enclosures
  • High-heat applications

PETG (Polyethylene Terephthalate Glycol)

Chemical-resistant and durable material that combines the ease of printing of PLA with the strength and flexibility of ABS. Excellent layer adhesion and impact resistance.

Print Temp
220-250°C
Bed Temp
70-80°C
Operating Temp
-40°C to 70°C
Best For:
  • Water bottles and containers
  • Mechanical parts
  • Outdoor applications
  • Chemical-resistant parts

TPU (Thermoplastic Polyurethane)

Flexible, rubber-like material that can bend and stretch without breaking. Available in different hardness levels (shore ratings) for various flexibility needs.

Print Temp
220-250°C
Bed Temp
40-60°C
Operating Temp
-40°C to 80°C
Best For:
  • Phone cases and grips
  • Flexible hinges and joints
  • Shoe soles and insoles
  • Cushions and dampeners

Nylon (Polyamide)

Strong, durable, and versatile engineering plastic known for its excellent mechanical properties. Good wear resistance and can be used for both functional and aesthetic parts.

Print Temp
240-270°C
Bed Temp
80-100°C
Operating Temp
-40°C to 100°C
Best For:
  • Gears and mechanical parts
  • Tool handles and grips
  • Fasteners and clips
  • Durable functional prototypes

Resin Materials for SLA Printing

Photopolymer resins cured by UV light for high-detail, smooth surface finish printing

Standard Resin

General-purpose photopolymer resin that provides excellent detail and surface finish. Balanced properties for most applications with good dimensional accuracy.

Layer Height
0.025-0.1mm
Cure Time
2-5 min
Operating Temp
-10°C to 60°C
Best For:
  • Detailed miniatures and models
  • Architectural models
  • Jewelry prototyping
  • General prototyping

Tough Resin

Impact-resistant and durable resin designed for functional parts that need to withstand stress and impact. Higher tensile strength compared to standard resin.

Layer Height
0.025-0.1mm
Cure Time
3-6 min
Operating Temp
-10°C to 60°C
Best For:
  • Functional prototypes
  • Tool handles and grips
  • Durable parts and fixtures
  • Impact-resistant components

Flexible Resin

Bendable and elastic photopolymer that can flex and return to its original shape. Ideal for applications requiring flexibility and shock absorption.

Layer Height
0.025-0.1mm
Cure Time
4-8 min
Operating Temp
-20°C to 50°C
Best For:
  • Flexible hinges and joints
  • Gaskets and seals
  • Phone cases and protectors
  • Shock-absorbing components

Castable Resin

Specially formulated resin designed to burn out cleanly during the lost-wax casting process. Used for creating detailed metal parts through investment casting.

Layer Height
0.025-0.05mm
Cure Time
2-4 min
Operating Temp
-10°C to 60°C
Best For:
  • Jewelry and metal casting patterns
  • Detailed metal parts
  • Investment casting masters
  • Fine detail metal components

Clear Resin

Transparent photopolymer resin that produces clear, glass-like finished parts. Excellent optical clarity for applications requiring transparency.

Layer Height
0.025-0.05mm
Cure Time
2-5 min
Operating Temp
-10°C to 60°C
Best For:
  • Optical components and lenses
  • Light guides and displays
  • Transparent prototypes
  • Fluid flow visualization models

Bio-Compatible Resin

Medical-grade photopolymer resin designed for applications requiring direct contact with human tissue or fluids. Meets biocompatibility standards and is suitable for medical and dental applications.

Layer Height
0.025-0.05mm
Cure Time
3-7 min
Operating Temp
-10°C to 60°C
Best For:
  • Dental models and appliances
  • Medical device prototypes
  • Surgical guides and templates
  • Orthodontic applications
  • Custom medical implants

Material Selection Guide

Quick guide to help you choose the right material for your project

FDM vs Resin Printing

FDM (Filament)
  • Pros: Lower cost, wider material variety, easier post-processing
  • Cons: Visible layer lines, lower detail
  • Best for: Functional parts, larger objects
Resin (SLA)
  • Pros: High detail, smooth surface, isotropic strength
  • Cons: Higher cost, limited material variety, requires UV curing
  • Best for: Detailed models, miniatures, jewelry
